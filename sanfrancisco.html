This city holds history. It was originally named Yerba Buena when the area was owned by Mexico. The name changed when the United States acquired the land in 1846. Many tourists enjoy visiting for vacation. One stop that seems to make everyone’s list is the Golden Gate Bridge. The bridge was built in four years in 1937 and painted in the color: International Orange. On a trip to the city, Mark Twain described it as, “The coldest winter I ever spent was a summer in San Francisco.” Some of the tourists are well-known actors, directors, artists, and icons. Lee Jun-Fan or more commonly known as Bruce Lee was born in the city, who then grew up to be a world famous actor, director, martial artist, and philosopher. Alongside famous people, San Francisco is the home of multiple inventions. Some of which include: denim jeans, slot machines, jukeboxes, bendy straws, martinis, popsicles, fortune cookies, electric tvs, and the water bed.
